# Revolut Financial Stability & FinTech Risk Analysis

## Overview
This project analyzes Revolut’s evolution as a digital-first neobank and evaluates its
financial performance, business model, and financial stability risks in the digital banking era.

The analysis is based on academic research and publicly available financial data
from 2019–2024.

## Key Areas Analyzed
- Neobank business model and revenue streams
- Financial performance trends (revenue, profitability, deposits)
- Impact of COVID-19 on FX and transactional revenues
- Regulatory and licensing challenges
- Crypto exposure and macroeconomic risks
- Contribution to financial inclusion and systemic risk

## Tools & Methods
- Financial trend analysis
- Comparative banking model analysis
- FinTech risk assessment
- Desk research using regulatory and industry sources

## Key Insights
- Revenue grew from £166M (2019) to £2.1B (2024) with profitability achieved in 2023
- Heavy reliance on crypto and transaction-based income introduces volatility
- Regulatory delays (UK banking license) remain a major constraint
- Global expansion increases compliance and operational risk
